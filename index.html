Blackjack Rules Explained

The goal of blackjack is to beat the dealer by getting a hand value closest to 21 without exceeding it. Here’s how it works:

  • Card Values: 2-10 are face value, face cards (J, Q, K) are 10, Aces are 1 or 11.
  • Game Flow: Players get two cards; dealer gets one face-up, one face-down.
  • Actions: Hit, stand, double down, split pairs, or surrender (if available).
  • Winning: Beat the dealer without busting. Blackjack (Ace + 10-value card) pays 3:2.

Winning Strategies for Blackjack

Blackjack rewards skill over luck. Here are key strategies to boost your odds:

Basic Strategy

Use a strategy chart to make optimal decisions based on your hand and the dealer’s upcard:

  • Stand on 17+.
  • Hit on 8 or less.
  • Double on 11 vs. dealer’s 2-10.
  • Split Aces and 8s; avoid splitting 10s.

Card Counting

Track high/low cards (e.g., Hi-Lo system: +1 for 2-6, 0 for 7-9, -1 for 10-Ace). Less effective online due to shuffles.

Bankroll Management

Bet 1-2% of your bankroll per hand, set loss limits, and stick to them.
